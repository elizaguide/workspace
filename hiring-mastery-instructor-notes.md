## Section 3: The Job Quadrant Framework - THE CORE (20 minutes)

### Background Story
Share how Vishen discovered this during his interview process and how it aligns perfectly with Maslow's hierarchy.

### The Four Quadrants

| **Job Quadrant** | **Maslow Level** | **What It Really Means** | **Red Flags** |
|------------------|------------------|--------------------------|---------------|
| **Friendship** | Connection & Belonging | Seeks meaningful relationships at work | "I work alone," antisocial tendencies |
| **Abundance** | Self-Esteem | Wants respect and recognition (*not just money*) | "Money is everything," no intrinsic motivation |
| **Growth** | Self-Actualization | Committed to personal development | "I'm fine as I am," fixed mindset |
| **Meaning** | Self-Transcendence | Wants to contribute to something bigger | "It's just a job," transactional thinking |

### ðŸ’Ž CRITICAL INSIGHT TO EMPHASIZE
**"Abundance was never about money. Rather it was about the respect that people felt money gave them. The money was a means goal."**

#### How to Use This in Interviews:
**The Key Question:** *"What matters most to you in a job - is it the mission, personal growth, financial security, or the relationships you'll build?"*

### Ideal Candidates Choose:
1. **Growth + Meaning** (Perfect fit - naturally transformational)
2. **Friendship + Growth** (Great fit - will thrive in collaborative culture)
3. **Meaning + Abundance** (Good fit - mission-driven but needs recognition)

### Red Flag Combinations:
- **Abundance only** (Money-focused, likely to job-hop)
- **Friendship + Abundance** (Social but transactional)
- **No clear preference** (Lacks self-awareness)

## Section 4: The 3MIQ Process for New Hires (15 minutes)

### What is 3MIQ?
**Three Most Important Questions** - A framework where new employees define their personal dreams and goals.

### The Implementation Process:
1. **During onboarding:** All new hires complete their 3MIQ
2. **Public sharing:** Posted on bulletin board for team visibility  
3. **Manager access:** Copies shared with direct managers
4. **CEO connection:** Vishen keeps photos of every employee's 3MIQ on his phone
5. **Regular check-ins:** Used in one-on-one meetings and career development

### The Power of 3MIQ:
- **Visibility beyond the faÃ§ade:** See who they really are
- **Team bonding:** When everyone knows each other's 3MIQs, deeper connections form
- **Career development:** Leaders can support employees' personal dreams
- **Retention:** People stay longer when they feel seen and supported

### Success Story to Share:
**Luminita Saviuc** - Joined as customer support agent from Romania. Her 3MIQ included "become a published author and speak internationally." While at Mindvalley, she wrote "15 Things to Give Up to Be Happy" which went viral (1.2M Facebook shares), leading to a Penguin Random House book deal.
