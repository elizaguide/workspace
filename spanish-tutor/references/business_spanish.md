## Common Business Phrases

### Expressing Opinion
- **En mi opinión...** - In my opinion...
- **Desde mi perspectiva...** - From my perspective...
- **Creo que...** - I believe that...
- **Me parece que...** - It seems to me that...
- **Estoy convencido de que...** - I'm convinced that...

### Agreement & Disagreement
- **Estoy completamente de acuerdo** - I completely agree
- **Tiene razón** - You're right
- **Exactamente** - Exactly
- **No estoy seguro de eso** - I'm not sure about that
- **Veo su punto, pero...** - I see your point, but...

### Making Proposals
- **Propongo que...** - I propose that...
- **¿Qué le parece si...?** - What do you think if...?
- **Sugiero que...** - I suggest that...
- **Podríamos considerar...** - We could consider...

### Time & Scheduling
- **¿Cuándo le viene bien?** - When is convenient for you?
- **¿Está libre el...?** - Are you free on...?
- **Confirmemos la reunión** - Let's confirm the meeting
- **¿Podemos reagendar?** - Can we reschedule?

## Cultural Business Tips

### Spain vs Latin America
- **Spain**: More direct communication, shorter business relationships
- **Mexico**: Relationship-building crucial, more formal initially
- **Argentina**: European influence, sophisticated business culture
- **Colombia**: Warm relationships, family often discussed

### Formal vs Informal
- Use **"usted"** initially in all business contexts
- Switch to **"tú"** only when invited
- **"Don/Doña"** for very senior executives
- Business cards exchanged with both hands

### Meeting Etiquette
- Arrive on time or slightly late (5-10 minutes)
- Greet everyone individually
- Small talk before business
- Lunch meetings are relationship-building time
